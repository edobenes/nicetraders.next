Frequently Asked Questions

When you travel overseas you will mostly pay with your credit card. But cash is always handy for buying rides on public transit, tips, paying street vendors for food, and craft items and small incidentals. Getting cash at exchange kiosks is surprisingly expensive. Even ATMs charge a fee, and you have to wait until you get to your destination country hoping find one. Selling back any currency you still have is not only costly again, but it is also sometimes impossible to do.

From as far back at 2013, USA Today reported:

“Indeed, according to a recent poll of 2,105 American vacationers by www.forexcurrency.us, respondents brought back an average of $32 in unspent foreign funds from their international trips — and 83% conceded they never exchanged the money back to U.S. dollars. (When asked why, 54% said they just didn't get around to it and 35% said there was "no point." And while 11% said they were saving the loot for their next trip, a quarter of those said it would probably be left behind.)’

You tell us what currency you want to buy, what currency you plan to pay with, and how much you want. Your request goes into our worldwide database, and we identify reciprocal NICE Traders in your neighborhood. When you find one you want to trade with, we double check to confirm they still want to trade and are willing to trade with you. When you have both committed to trade, you each pay us one dollar, we put you in touch with each other via mutually agreeable methods, and you arrange to meet and exchange your currency.

This is another exciting part! The market rate, also known as the interbank rate, is the exchange rate at which banks borrow from and lend to each other. Usually, the market rate is different from the customer rate. The customer rate is the market rate plus the profit margin of the financial company you are dealing with. In other words, you pay more for currency than it is worth. Your home bank, AAA, a money exchanger at the airport, etc will tack on 5-7%. And some money changers (including ATMs) will charge an additional fee.

NICE Traders is different. The exchange rate you pay through us is the exact exchange rate giant international banks use with each other; i.e. the market rate. There is no premium paid or charged. The rate you pay when you buy your currency is the same rate you pay when you sell it to another NICE Trader after you return home. Or, when NICE Traders goes international, you can sell it to another person before you even come home. Nothing is skimmed off the top. We charge a $1 fee (see “How much does it cost?” question below). Of course if you’ve held currency for a long time, the market rate may be different from when you bought, it, but the difference is due to international conditions, not NICE Traders.

  1. NICE Traders is free to sign up.
  2. NICE Traders is free to search.
  3. NICE Traders is free to keep your search active for a full year. After that if you want to continue with the search, it’s free to continue.
  4. NICE Traders is free to do multiple searches and keep multiple searches active. For example, if you have traveled to many countries and have numerous stashes of leftover currency, you can put them all up for sale for free.
  5. When you actually agree to make a trade, it costs you one dollar.

You are allowed to sell any currency at all. Whether you can depends on whether there is a NICE Trader nearby who wants to buy it. Just because you have currency from a country not frequently traveled does not mean it won’t sell. Plus, it doesn’t cost you anything to add your currency to the list of those in our database. People may even view your less common money to be a cool gift for a grandchild, niece, or nephew.

Large amounts of cash are restricted by the U.S. Treasury. To keep within government guidelines, NICE Traders restricts trades to less than $1000/day.

If you have more than $1000, simply create multiple trades.
